Biography

Kendrick Lamar Duckworth (born June 17, 1987) is an American rapper, songwriter, and record producer. Since his mainstream debut in 2012 with Good Kid, M.A.A.D City, Lamar has been regarded as one of the most influential artists of his generation, as well as one of the greatest rappers and lyricists of all time. Aside from his solo career, he is also known as a member of the hip hop supergroup Black Hippy alongside his Top Dawg Entertainment (TDE) label-mates Ab-Soul, Jay Rock, and Schoolboy Q.

Raised in Compton, California, Lamar embarked on his musical career as a teenager under the stage name K-Dot, releasing a mixtape that gained local attention and led to his signing with indie record label Top Dawg Entertainment (TDE). He began to gain recognition in 2010 after his first retail release, Overly Dedicated. The following year, he independently released his first studio album, Section.80, which included his debut single "HiiiPoWeR". By that time, he had amassed a large online following and collaborated with several prominent hip hop artists.

Lamar's major-label debut album, Good Kid, M.A.A.D City, was released in 2012 to critical acclaim. It was nominated for Album of the Year at the 56th Annual Grammy Awards. In 2015, his third album To Pimp a Butterfly was released to universal acclaim and was regarded as one of the defining works of the 2010s decade, as well as being his most successful album commercially, with sales of over 1 million copies in the US. In 2016, Lamar released Untitled Unmastered, a collection of unreleased demos that originated during the recording sessions for Butterfly. He released his fourth album, Damn, in 2017 to further acclaim; its lead single "Humble" topped the US Billboard Hot 100 chart.

Lamar has received many accolades over the course of his career, including 13 Grammy Awards. Time magazine named him one of the 100 most influential people in the world in 2016 and 2018. In 2018, Lamar won a Pulitzer Prize for his album Damn, becoming the first non-classical or jazz artist to receive the award. In 2020, he released the non-album single "The Heart Part 4", which contained lyrics expressing his dissatisfaction with the state of the world and teasing a release date of April 7. However, no album was released on that date.
